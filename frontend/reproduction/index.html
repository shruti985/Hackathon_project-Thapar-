Legal Rights & Information

Know your rights and legal protections

Reproductive Rights

Right to Privacy

Your medical information and reproductive choices are protected by privacy laws.

Access to Information

You have the right to accurate, unbiased medical information about all your options.

Freedom from Discrimination

Healthcare providers cannot discriminate based on age, marital status, or other factors.

Legal Protections

Confidentiality Laws

Medical providers are legally required to maintain confidentiality of your reproductive health information.

Informed Consent

You must be provided with complete information about any procedure, including risks and alternatives.

Emergency Care

Emergency departments are required to provide stabilizing care regardless of payment ability.

Legal Disclaimer

This information is for educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Laws vary by location and are subject to change. For specific legal questions, consult with a qualified attorney in your jurisdiction.

Testing Guidelines

Regular testing is key to maintaining sexual health.

Annual testing: All sexually active individuals

Every 3-6 months: Multiple partners or high-risk behaviors

Before new partners: Both partners should test
